I've been thinking about what kind of pages belong on a personal website that aren't blog posts and aren't portfolio entries. Something in between — deeper than a bookmark, more permanent than a tweet, more personal than a wiki article. The answer, for me, is a **folio**.

## What is a Folio?

Not a portfolio. Not a blog post. The word comes from bookbinding — a single sheet folded to make four pages, a self-contained document about one thing.

A folio is a handcrafted web page dedicated to a single subject you care about — designed visually to match that subject, living at a permanent URL, growing over time.

The key distinction from other things on this site:

- **A blog post** is dated, linear, finished
- **A portfolio** shows what you built professionally
- **A folio** shows what you love, obsess over, return to — it's curatorial, not promotional

Each folio gets its own design. Its own fonts, its own palette, its own layout. They share a domain but nothing else. The design is part of the meaning.

## The First Folio: Radiohead

The first one is a [Radiohead web archive](/folio/radiohead/) — a curated inventory of the band's internet presence: official sites, the thirteen archive maze sites (designed with Stanley Donwood), fan databases, essential albums, B-sides, live recordings, and personal notes.

It has its own aesthetic — dark background, typewriter fonts, gold and rust accents, scan-line textures, elements slightly off-kilter. It feels the way clicking through Radiohead's actual old sites felt: deliberate, a little broken, a little cryptic.

Some things I found while building it:

- **Sites 5, 6, and 11** of the archive maze are gone (404). The rest still work, including Site 13 — the "ur-maze" of discarded ephemera.
- **Site 7** links to a Friends of the Earth climate campaign page. Kid A energy.
- **Site 9** says "HAIL TO THE THIEF" and links to a Flash-era labyrinth.
- **Site 10** says "TEMPORARY RESTRAINING ORDER" and contains exactly one link.
- The [Kid A Mnesia Exhibition](https://kida-mnesia.com) is a free interactive experience that lets you walk through the artwork. It's not quite a game, not quite an album — it's the space between.

## How AI Fits In

AI removes the two things that made this kind of personal web presence impractical before: design skill and time.

**What AI handles:** generating the initial design from a description and aesthetic reference, iterating fast ("darker", "more zine-like", "add a notes section"), scaffolding the structure from raw links and notes.

**What you handle:** the taste (what subjects, what aesthetic fits), the content (actual links, notes, discoveries), the curation (what stays, what gets cut), the voice.

The workflow: you bring an obsession and raw material → AI builds a first-pass folio → you react and direct → AI iterates in minutes → you keep the content layer. Over months you accumulate folios that couldn't exist without this loop. The AI is a frontend collaborator. You're the editor and author.

## What's Next

More folios, each looking nothing like the last. The `/folio/` index will grow. The Radiohead one will deepen — it's the kind of page that's never finished, which feels appropriate for a band whose web presence was built on the idea that a website is just another medium, as temporary and strange as any vinyl pressing or screenprint.
