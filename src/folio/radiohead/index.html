note to self

I started collecting these links the way you start any obsession — one click led to another and suddenly it was 3am and I was deep inside Site 13 trying to figure out if a broken Flash animation was intentional or just old. Most of it was intentional. That's the thing about Radiohead's web presence — it was never just a website. It was the artwork continuing by other means.

the dead & the lost
Sites that were real, that people used, that are now gone or Wayback-only. Listed here so they're not forgotten.
✕
ateaseweb.com/board
The At Ease message board. ~20k daily visitors at its peak. The band pointed fans here. Server stopped paying bills August 2024.
[board dead]
✕
mortigitempo.com
Green Plastic's message board, became its own community. More welcoming than At Ease. Central during Kid A through TKOL. Now effectively gone.
[offline]
✕
58hours.com
Radiohead gig database with a world-map interface. Used as the canonical setlist source for years. Gone around 2022.
[offline]
✕
msgboard.radiohead.com
The official band message board. Still resolves, but overrun by spam. A relic.
[spam ruin]
✕
followmearound.com
The fan campaign that pressured Thom to debut "Follow Me Around" live — it worked. The song finally appeared on Kid A Mnesia. Site's gone.
[offline]
✕
lift (site)
Jonathan Moore's early fan site. Green Plastic's Cold Storage preserves a facsimile specifically because the original is gone.
[wayback only]
note

The internet of 1997–2012 was a different place. These sites built communities and transmitted culture that the band's official presence never could. Their loss is a real loss.

section 03
the music — a way in
Kid A Mnesia Exhibition
A free interactive game/experience built by Arbitrarily Good Productions and Epic Games. Walk through the artwork of Kid A and Amnesiac. Available on PC, Mac, and PS5. It's not a game, it's not an album, it's the space between.
interactive official
kida-mnesia.com ↗
Pablo Honey
1993
They were a different band. Britrock, still finding it. "Creep" happened and they spent the next decade running from it. Worth knowing for where they started.
origin
The Bends
1995
The last album with guitars at the centre. "Fake Plastic Trees" and "Street Spirit" are devastating songs written by a band that hadn't figured out how to be devastating yet. You can hear them getting there.
gateway
OK Computer
1997
The one that broke everything open. "Fitter Happier" is a poem read by a text-to-speech engine and it's more human than most lyrics.
essential
Kid A
2000
They deleted themselves and became something else. No singles. No guitars on half the tracks. Still their best, probably.
essential
Amnesiac
2001
Kid A's shadow twin. Recorded in the same sessions but darker, jazzier, weirder. "Life in a Glasshouse" has Humphrey Lyttelton's band playing funeral jazz.
essential
Hail to the Thief
2003
The political one. "2+2=5" opened Glastonbury before the album was even out. Dense, loud, and underrated. "There There" is a career highlight.
underrated
In Rainbows
2007
Pay what you want. The warmest thing they ever made. "Reckoner" is perfect. "Videotape" hides its beat where you don't expect it.
essential
The King of Limbs
2011
Eight tracks of rhythm-first, loop-driven songwriting. The live versions are better. That's intentional — they built it to be taken apart on stage.
A Moon Shaped Pool
2016
After the divorce. "True Love Waits" — written 21 years earlier — finally recorded, solo piano. The saddest thing in their catalogue.
essential
Kid A Mnesia
2021
Kid A + Amnesiac reissued together with a third disc, "Kid Amnesiae" — unreleased recordings from the sessions. "Follow Me Around" is finally here. Plus the Exhibition game.
reissue

The real gateway isn't an album. It's "In Rainbows: From the Basement" — they played the whole thing live in a studio and it's some of the best live performance footage ever recorded. Watch on YouTube ↗

b-sides & deep cuts
Talk Show Host
B-side from the Street Spirit single. Used in Romeo + Juliet. Darker and slower than anything on The Bends. Trip-hop before they committed to it.
b-sidethe bends era
The Daily Mail / Staircase
Two tracks released standalone in 2011. The Daily Mail is a piano-driven protest song that builds to a wall of sound. Staircase is a hypnotic loop. Both are better than half of TKOL.
standalone2011
Spectre
Written for the Bond film. Rejected. Released on Christmas Day 2015. Orchestral, haunting, and arguably better than the Sam Smith song they went with.
standalone2015
I Might Be Wrong: Live Recordings
Mini live album from the Kid A / Amnesiac tours. "Like Spinning Plates" live is a completely different song — piano and voice, devastating. Essential.
liveessential
Glastonbury 2003
The legendary headline set. They opened with "2+2=5" before the album was out. Closed with "Everything in Its Right Place" in the rain. Widely bootlegged, pieces on YouTube.
live
personal note

If someone asks where to start: In Rainbows if they want beauty, OK Computer if they want ambition, Kid A if they want to skip straight to the part where everything changes. But honestly — find the From the Basement sessions. That's the real introduction.

scrapbooks, blogs, notes
Dead Air Space
archive.radiohead.com/Site12/deadairspace/

Thom Yorke's blog from the In Rainbows and King of Limbs years. Studio dispatches, cryptic playlist scrawls, notes about canal boats and modular synthesis and tax investigations and global warming. Probably the most human thing they ever published.

official archive
Diet of Worms
via radiohead.com/library → Memory Hole

The 2004 online scrapbook. Yorke dumped unused lyrics here then returned during writer's block. Burn the Witch was in here a decade before the album. Doodles, old photos, early-era fragments.

official archive maze
Stanley Donwood
instagram.com/stanleydonwood

The artist behind every piece of Radiohead artwork since The Bends. The maze sites are his. The OK Computer sketchbook is his. Worth following on its own terms — the process work is extraordinary.

donwood artwork

The word "Tchock" — Yorke's pseudonym — appears throughout the archive sites. Just a sound. Not an anagram of anything. Donwood and Tchock made art together the way other people make dinner — regularly, without ceremony, and the results accumulated into something extraordinary.

fan sites & deep archives

Every album, every side project, full discography, media coverage, live performances, videos. Made in Germany. Est. 2006. Probably the most complete reference that exists.

Oldest US fan site. Launched March 1997. Frozen around 2012 but preserved — gear info, print articles, a facsimile of the lost site "Lift", two decades of fan art.

Et Cetera
Launched 2025 to replace At Ease and Mortigi Tempo. Proper forum structure, no karma, no algorithm. Already 2,000+ members, 14k music posts. The spiritual successor to the classic boards.

The message board is gone — server went down August 2024. But the news pages and live archive (e.g. "live 2004") still resolve. Had ~20,000 daily visitors in the OK Computer / Kid A years. The band pointed fans here.

last thought

This page will never be finished, which feels appropriate. The band's whole web presence was built on the idea that a website is just another medium — as temporary and strange as any vinyl pressing or screenprint. The links will break. Some already have. That's part of it.
